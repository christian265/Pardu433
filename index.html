Start early

You're going to want to start planning your trip well in advance. The sooner you get started, the more options you'll have, and you'll also be able to secure better deals on airfare and hotels.

Another thing to keep in mind is the weather. India is a huge country, and the weather can vary dramatically depending on which region you're visiting. So do your homework and make sure you pack accordingly.

And finally, don't be afraid to explore. India is an amazing country with a rich culture and an incredible history. There's so much to see and do, so don't hesitate to venture off the beaten path.

Stay safe

When you're traveling in a foreign country, safety should always be your number one priority. India is a beautiful country with a rich history and culture, but it can also be dangerous if you're not careful.

There are a few things you can do to stay safe while you're in India: always stay aware of your surroundings, don't carry too much cash with you, and avoid walking around at night. If you're going to be out late, take a taxi or an Uber instead of walking.

And if you do happen to get lost, don't panic! Just find a busy area with lots of people and ask for help. People in India are generally very friendly and will be happy to assist you.

Dress conservatively

When traveling in India, it's important to dress conservatively. That doesn't mean you have to cover up from head to toe, but you should avoid wearing anything too revealing.

In fact, many women choose to wear a sari, which is a traditional Indian dress that covers most of your body. If you're not comfortable wearing a sari, there are plenty of other options, like pants and long-sleeved shirts.

Just be sure to avoid tight clothing or anything that shows too much skin. You'll want to respect the local culture and dress in a way that's appropriate for the country you're visiting.
